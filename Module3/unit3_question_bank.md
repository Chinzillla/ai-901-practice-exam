## Unit 3 Question Bank: Statistical text analysis

These questions are authored from the unit's Microsoft Learn concepts and rewritten as exam-style practice items. Answer choices are intentionally paraphrased instead of copied from the lesson text.

### 1. A team wants to identify the most common terms in one support article. Which technique fits best?

A. TF-IDF  
B. bag-of-words  
C. TextRank  
D. Frequency analysis  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** The requirement matches the unit capability named in the correct option.

---

### 2. What is the main purpose of "frequency analysis"?

A. representing text by word counts or occurrences without preserving grammar or word order  
B. ranking words or sentences by graph relationships to identify important content  
C. counting token occurrences to infer common topics or themes  
D. scoring terms higher when they are frequent in one document but uncommon across many documents  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** "frequency analysis" is a core idea in this unit, and the correct option paraphrases its role.

### 3. A search system should highlight terms that are distinctive to one document, not common everywhere. What should it use?

A. Speech synthesis  
B. TF-IDF scoring  
C. Prompt engineering  
D. Object detection  

**Answer:** B  
**Rationale:** The correct option is the unit concept that best fits the stated requirement.

### 4. Why can simple frequency analysis be weaker than TF-IDF across many documents?

A. Frequency alone can overvalue common words; TF-IDF highlights terms that are distinctive in a collection.  
B. TF-IDF only works for speech audio.  
C. Frequency analysis creates images while TF-IDF creates video.  
D. Both ignore how often words appear.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The unit contrasts raw counts with term importance across a corpus.

### 5. What should a learner remember about "TF-IDF"?

A. a configuration task for the interface rather than the AI behavior  
B. a rule-based process that does not use model reasoning  
C. an access-control setting rather than the concept being defined  
D. ranking terms higher when they are frequent in one document but uncommon across many documents  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** "TF-IDF" is tested by recognizing its purpose, not by memorizing wording from the page.

### 6. What is the purpose of a bag-of-words representation?

A. To locate objects inside an image.  
B. To remove all tokens before analysis.  
C. To turn text into numeric word features that a model can use.  
D. To preserve exact sentence grammar for voice synthesis.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** Bag-of-words is a feature extraction technique for text.
