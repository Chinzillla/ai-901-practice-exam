# Module 12: Implement a Responsible Generative AI Solution in Microsoft Foundry

## Unit 6 Question Bank: Manage a responsible generative AI solution

These questions are authored from the unit's Microsoft Learn concepts and rewritten as exam-style practice items. Answer choices are intentionally paraphrased instead of copied from the lesson text.

---

### 1. After release, a team reviews logs and user feedback for unsafe model behavior. What activity is this?

A. responsible operations  
B. incident response  
C. continuous improvement  
D. Monitoring  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** The requirement matches the unit capability named in the correct option.

---

### 2. What is the main purpose of "responsible operations"?

A. the process for handling harmful or unexpected AI behavior  
B. updating controls and practices as risks, usage, and requirements change  
C. monitoring and governing an AI solution after deployment  
D. tracking solution behavior, quality, and risk signals over time  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** "responsible operations" is a core idea in this unit, and the correct option paraphrases its role.

---

### 3. A harmful output is reported and the team follows a defined escalation plan. What process is being used?

A. Speech synthesis  
B. Incident response capability  
C. Prompt engineering  
D. Object detection  

**Answer:** B  
**Rationale:** The correct option is the unit concept that best fits the stated requirement.

---

### 4. Why does responsible AI continue after deployment?

A. Real use can reveal new risks, quality issues, and needed changes.  
B. Deployment proves the model is permanently safe.  
C. Monitoring is only for billing totals.  
D. Responsible AI ends when the model endpoint is created.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The unit treats responsible management as ongoing.

---

### 5. What should a learner remember about "monitoring"?

A. a configuration task for the interface rather than the AI behavior  
B. a rule-based process that does not use model reasoning  
C. an access-control setting rather than the concept being defined  
D. monitoring behavior, quality, and risk signals over time  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** "monitoring" is tested by recognizing its purpose, not by memorizing wording from the page.

---

### 6. What should a team do when monitoring reveals a new harmful pattern?

A. Delete all feedback.  
B. Disable user communication.  
C. Investigate, apply appropriate mitigations, and verify the change.  
D. Ignore the pattern until the next course update.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** Responsible operation requires responding to issues.

---

### 7. Which two activities belong to responsible solution management? Choose two.

A. Stop monitoring after launch  
B. Hide known issues  
C. Remove accountability  
D. Review user feedback  
E. Update safeguards when risks change  

**Answer:** D, E  
**Rationale:** Ongoing management includes feedback and control updates.

---

### 8. A team updates prompts and filters as new risk patterns appear. What operating practice is this?

A. Continuous improvement capability  
B. Information extraction  
C. Model evaluation  
D. Choose a model based on popularity instead of task fit.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The scenario points to the unit capability described by the correct option.

---

### 9. Which three practices support responsible AI operations? Choose three.

A. Assume no misuse can happen  
B. Avoid logs and review  
C. Bypass privacy requirements  
D. Monitor outputs  
E. Respond to incidents  
F. Improve controls over time  

**Answer:** D, E, F  
**Rationale:** Responsible operation is a lifecycle practice.

### 10. Which description best matches "incident response"?

A. Use a visual analysis capability when the input is only text.  
B. Handle sensitive data without the required access controls.  
C. process for handling harmful or unexpected AI behavior  
D. Rely on one successful trial instead of broader evaluation.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** The correct answer paraphrases how the unit explains "incident response".
