# Module 12: Implement a Responsible Generative AI Solution in Microsoft Foundry

## Unit 3 Question Bank: Map potential harms

These questions are authored from the unit's Microsoft Learn concepts and rewritten as exam-style practice items. Answer choices are intentionally paraphrased instead of copied from the lesson text.

---

### 1. A team lists how an AI tutor could mislead students, exclude learners, or expose data. What activity is this?

A. harm mapping  
B. stakeholder impact  
C. misuse  
D. Mapping potential harms  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** This scenario matches Map potential harms as described in the unit.

---

### 2. What is the main purpose of "harm mapping"?

A. using the system in a way that was not intended and may cause harm  
B. a grouped type of risk such as safety, fairness, privacy, or misinformation  
C. identifying ways an AI solution could negatively affect people or organizations  
D. how different groups may be helped or harmed by the solution  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** "harm mapping" is a core idea in this unit, and the correct option paraphrases its role.

---

### 3. A team considers how different user groups could be affected by an AI assistant. Which concept applies?

A. Object detection  
B. Stakeholder impact for this scenario  
C. harm category  
D. Prompt engineering  

**Answer:** B  
**Rationale:** The correct option is the unit concept that best fits the stated requirement.

---

### 4. How is mapping harms different from mitigating harms?

A. Mapping identifies possible harms; mitigation applies controls to reduce them.  
B. Mapping deploys the model endpoint.  
C. Mitigation only writes documentation.  
D. They are the same step and require no review.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The responsible AI process separates identifying risks from reducing them.

---

### 5. What should a learner remember about "stakeholder impact"?

A. a process for changing the visual theme of the development portal  
B. a manual-only activity that does not use an AI model  
C. a security shortcut that removes the need for access controls  
D. how different groups may be helped or harmed by the solution as described in this unit  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** "stakeholder impact" is tested by recognizing its purpose, not by memorizing wording from the page.

---

### 6. Why should harm mapping include multiple stakeholders?

A. Stakeholder input prevents all testing.  
B. Harms are identical for every group.  
C. Different people may see different risks, impacts, and misuse paths.  
D. Only developers can identify user impact.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** Broader review improves risk identification.

---

### 7. Which two questions help map potential harms? Choose two.

A. What color is the portal header?  
B. Can we skip monitoring?  
C. How can we hide limitations?  
D. Who could be affected by the system?  
E. How could the system be misused?  

**Answer:** D, E  
**Rationale:** Harm mapping looks at affected groups and misuse.

---

### 8. Users might ask a model to generate prohibited advice outside the approved purpose. What risk is this?

A. Misuse for this scenario  
B. Speech synthesis  
C. Information extraction  
D. Model evaluation  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The scenario points to the unit capability described by the correct option.

---

### 9. Which three harm areas should teams consider? Choose three.

A. Keyboard color  
B. Window position  
C. File extension style only  
D. Fairness  
E. Privacy  
F. Safety  

**Answer:** D, E, F  
**Rationale:** Responsible AI harm mapping includes fairness, privacy, safety, and related risks.

### 10. Which description best matches "misuse"?

A. Skip testing because AI outputs are always deterministic.  
B. Use image analysis even when the input is only plain text.  
C. using the system in a way that was not intended and may cause harm as described in this unit  
D. Choose a model only because it has the largest name recognition.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** The correct answer paraphrases how the unit explains "misuse".
