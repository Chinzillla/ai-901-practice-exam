# Module 4: Introduction to AI Speech Concepts

## Unit 7 Question Bank: Summary

These questions provide cumulative review for Module 4.

---

### 1. What does speech recognition produce?

A. Text from spoken audio  
B. Audio from written text  
C. Image labels  
D. Document fields only  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech recognition converts audio into text.

---

### 2. What does speech synthesis produce?

A. Spoken audio from text  
B. Text from audio  
C. Image segments  
D. Entity labels only  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech synthesis converts text into audio.

---

### 3. Which concept is used in recognition feature extraction?

A. MFCC  
B. OCR  
C. Image classification  
D. Field mapping  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** MFCCs summarize speech audio characteristics.

---

### 4. Which concept helps speech sound natural?

A. Prosody  
B. Stop words  
C. Object detection  
D. File indexing  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Prosody controls rhythm, stress, pitch, and pauses.

---

### 5. A voice assistant listens and speaks back. Which capabilities are combined?

A. Speech recognition and speech synthesis  
B. OCR and image generation  
C. TF-IDF and segmentation  
D. File search and compression only  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** It needs both audio input understanding and spoken output.

---

### 6. True or False: Speech technologies can support accessibility.

**Answer:** True  
**Rationale:** Speech can help users consume content or interact hands-free.

---

### 7. True or False: Neural vocoders are associated with generating audio waveforms.

**Answer:** True  
**Rationale:** Vocoders generate final audio from acoustic representations.

---

### 8. True or False: Speech recognition and synthesis are unrelated to conversational AI.

**Answer:** False  
**Rationale:** Conversational AI often uses both capabilities for voice interaction.
