# Module 4: Introduction to AI Speech Concepts

## Unit 1: Introduction

### Source

Microsoft Learn: [Introduction](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/introduction-ai-speech/1-introduction)

### Unit Overview

This unit introduces speech as a natural way for users to interact with AI applications. Learners preview the two fundamental speech capabilities: speech recognition, which converts spoken words to text, and speech synthesis, which converts text to natural-sounding speech.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, learners should be able to:

- Explain why speech can improve AI application experiences.
- Define speech recognition at a high level.
- Define speech synthesis at a high level.
- Recognize how speech capabilities support accessibility and conversational AI.

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| AI speech | AI capabilities that process spoken input or generate spoken output. |
| Speech recognition | Converting spoken words into text. |
| Speech synthesis | Converting text into spoken audio. |
| Voice-enabled application | An application that supports spoken interaction. |

### Lesson Flow

#### 1. Why Speech Matters

Explain that speech is one of the most natural human communication methods. Adding speech to AI applications can make experiences more intuitive, accessible, and engaging.

#### 2. Speech Recognition

Introduce speech recognition as converting spoken language into text. This supports voice commands, transcription, and spoken interaction with agents.

#### 3. Speech Synthesis

Introduce speech synthesis as converting written text into natural-sounding speech. This supports spoken responses, accessibility, and audio content.

#### 4. Combined Voice Experiences

Explain that conversational systems often use both capabilities: recognizing user speech and synthesizing a spoken response.

### Suggested Activities

#### Activity 1: Voice Experience Brainstorm

Learners list applications where voice input or output improves usability.

#### Activity 2: Recognition or Synthesis

Learners classify examples as speech recognition, speech synthesis, or both.

### Checks for Understanding

1. What does speech recognition do?
2. What does speech synthesis do?
3. Why can speech improve accessibility?
4. When might an app use both speech capabilities?
