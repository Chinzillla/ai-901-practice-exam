# Module 4: Introduction to AI Speech Concepts

## Unit 1 Question Bank: Introduction

These questions reinforce foundational AI speech concepts.

---

### 1. What does speech recognition do?

A. Converts spoken words into text  
B. Converts images into captions  
C. Extracts fields from forms  
D. Classifies sentiment only  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech recognition turns spoken input into written text.

---

### 2. What does speech synthesis do?

A. Converts text into spoken audio  
B. Converts audio into written text  
C. Detects objects in images  
D. Extracts key terms from documents  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech synthesis produces spoken output from text.

---

### 3. Why can speech capabilities improve AI applications?

A. They create more natural and accessible interactions  
B. They remove all need for responsible AI  
C. They only work in databases  
D. They prevent users from communicating  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech can support hands-free, accessible, and conversational experiences.

---

### 4. A chatbot hears a spoken question and answers aloud. Which capabilities are used?

A. Speech recognition and speech synthesis  
B. Image classification and OCR  
C. TF-IDF and TextRank only  
D. File compression and routing  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The chatbot must convert speech to text and text back to speech.

---

### 5. True or False: Speech recognition and speech synthesis are the same capability.

**Answer:** False  
**Rationale:** Recognition handles spoken input; synthesis creates spoken output.

---

### 6. True or False: Speech can support accessibility in AI applications.

**Answer:** True  
**Rationale:** Speech can help users who benefit from spoken input, audio output, or hands-free interaction.
