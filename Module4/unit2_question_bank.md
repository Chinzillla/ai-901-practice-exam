# Module 4: Introduction to AI Speech Concepts

## Unit 2 Question Bank: Speech-Enabled Solutions

These questions test practical understanding of speech-enabled solution scenarios, combined speech recognition and synthesis workflows, business value, and implementation considerations.

---

### 1. A service center wants agents to see a live written record while customers speak on the phone. Which capability is the best fit?

A. Speech synthesis  
B. Speech recognition  
C. Image generation  
D. Text normalization  

**Answer:** B  
**Rationale:** Speech recognition, also called speech-to-text, transcribes spoken audio into written text for scenarios such as call center support.

---

### 2. A navigation app needs to announce turn-by-turn directions while the driver keeps their eyes on the road. Which capability is being used?

A. Text-to-speech output  
B. Speech-to-text transcription  
C. Sentiment analysis  
D. Acoustic modeling  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech synthesis, also called text-to-speech, converts written or system-generated text into spoken audio.

---

### 3. A company wants an AI phone agent that listens to a customer question and then speaks a helpful response. Which combination is required?

A. Transcription of the caller only  
B. Spoken response generation only  
C. Speech-to-text for input and text-to-speech for output  
D. Computer vision for input and image generation for output  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** A two-way conversational voice solution uses recognition to capture spoken input and synthesis to produce spoken output.

---

### 4. Which two examples are speech recognition scenarios? Choose two.

A. Creating searchable meeting notes from a recorded conversation  
B. Reading a web article aloud for a visually impaired user  
C. Dictating patient notes into an electronic health record  
D. Generating a character voice for a game  
E. Announcing GPS directions to a driver  

**Answer:** A, C  
**Rationale:** Meeting transcription and clinical dictation convert speech into text. The other options generate audio from text or content.

---

### 5. Which two examples are speech synthesis scenarios? Choose two.

A. Routing a caller based on what they say  
B. Providing audio versions of written course materials  
C. Creating voiceovers for videos or presentations  
D. Extracting sentiment from a call transcript  
E. Capturing patient details from spoken dictation  

**Answer:** B, C  
**Rationale:** Speech synthesis creates spoken audio from written text, such as narrated learning materials or voiceovers.

---

### 6. Why should a team evaluate microphone quality, background noise, and network bandwidth before adding speech recognition?

A. They affect recognition accuracy and downstream transcription quality.  
B. They determine whether text-to-speech can expand abbreviations.  
C. They replace the need for privacy and compliance review.  
D. They are only relevant for image-based AI workloads.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Audio quality factors such as background noise, microphone quality, and network conditions can affect speech recognition accuracy.

---

### 7. A global customer support app must support callers in multiple regions. Which implementation consideration is most directly relevant?

A. Language and dialect support  
B. Whether the app can generate images  
C. Whether all users must use voice only  
D. Whether speech synthesis can remove background noise  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Speech solutions should verify target language and regional dialect support before implementation.

---

### 8. Which three benefits can speech capabilities add to AI applications? Choose three.

A. Support users with visual impairments or mobility challenges  
B. Enable hands-free multitasking  
C. Create more natural conversational experiences  
D. Guarantee that every transcription is legally valid without review  
E. Eliminate the need for text-based alternatives  
F. Support multiple languages and regional audiences  

**Answer:** A, B, C  
**Rationale:** Speech can improve accessibility, productivity, and user experience. Text alternatives and compliance review may still be required.

---

### 9. Why should speech-enabled applications still provide text-based alternatives?

A. Some users may prefer or require non-speech input or output.  
B. Speech synthesis cannot be used in conversational AI.  
C. Speech recognition only works for entertainment scenarios.  
D. Text alternatives prevent the use of multiple languages.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The unit emphasizes that speech should not create barriers; alternative input and output methods are important for accessibility and user preference.

---

### 10. A real-time voice assistant must answer quickly during a conversation, while a nightly batch transcription job can wait longer. Which consideration explains the difference?

A. Latency expectations  
B. Phoneme count  
C. Image resolution  
D. Document layout  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Real-time conversations require low latency, while batch transcription can tolerate more delay.
