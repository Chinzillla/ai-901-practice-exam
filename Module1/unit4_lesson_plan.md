# Module 1: Introduction to AI Concepts

## Unit 4: Speech

### Source

Microsoft Learn: [Speech](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/modules/get-started-ai-fundamentals/4-speech)

### Unit Overview

This unit introduces AI speech capabilities. Learners explore how AI applications and agents can support spoken interaction through speech recognition and speech synthesis. The unit also connects speech capabilities to common scenarios such as spoken assistants, meeting transcription, audio descriptions, and speech translation.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, learners should be able to:

- Define AI speech capabilities.
- Explain the difference between speech recognition and speech synthesis.
- Identify speech-to-text as a common form of speech recognition.
- Identify text-to-speech as a common form of speech synthesis.
- Recognize common AI speech scenarios.
- Choose speech capabilities for business scenarios involving spoken input or spoken output.

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| AI speech | AI capabilities that allow applications or agents to work with spoken language. |
| Speech recognition | The ability of AI to interpret spoken language, often by converting audio into text. |
| Speech-to-text | A speech recognition capability that transcribes spoken audio into written text. |
| Speech synthesis | The ability of AI to generate spoken language from text. |
| Text-to-speech | A speech synthesis capability that converts written text into audible speech. |
| Speech translation | Converting spoken language from one language into another. |
| Transcription | Converting spoken audio, such as a meeting or call, into text. |

### Lesson Flow

#### 1. Introduce AI Speech

Explain that AI speech capabilities allow people to interact with applications and agents using spoken language. This can make AI systems more natural and accessible, especially when users cannot or do not want to type.

Example: a computing history site could let users ask questions through a microphone and then hear the response as spoken audio.

#### 2. Speech Recognition

Define speech recognition as the ability of AI to interpret spoken language. Emphasize that speech recognition commonly appears as speech-to-text, where an audio signal is transcribed into written text.

Examples:

- Transcribing a customer support call.
- Turning a spoken question into text before sending it to a chatbot.
- Capturing meeting notes from spoken discussion.

Key teaching point: speech recognition handles spoken input.

#### 3. Speech Synthesis

Define speech synthesis as the ability of AI to produce spoken language. Emphasize that speech synthesis commonly appears as text-to-speech, where written text is converted into audible speech.

Examples:

- Reading an answer aloud to a user.
- Creating spoken descriptions from text.
- Providing voice responses in an AI assistant.

Key teaching point: speech synthesis handles spoken output.

#### 4. Practical Challenges in Speech AI

Briefly discuss that speech systems must handle real-world audio conditions. These can include background noise, interruptions, different voices, and the need for natural-sounding output.

Keep this section high level. Learners do not need to understand the engineering details for this introductory unit.

#### 5. Common AI Speech Scenarios

Review common uses of speech technologies:

- AI agents that understand spoken input and respond with speech.
- Automated transcription of calls or meetings.
- Audio descriptions of video or text.
- Speech translation between languages.

Key teaching point: if the scenario involves spoken input, spoken output, transcription, or spoken translation, speech AI is likely involved.

### Suggested Activities

#### Activity 1: Recognition or Synthesis

Give learners scenarios and ask whether they require speech recognition, speech synthesis, or both.

Examples:

- A meeting recording is converted into text. Speech recognition.
- A chatbot reads its answer aloud. Speech synthesis.
- A voice assistant hears a question and answers out loud. Both.

#### Activity 2: Match the Capability

Ask learners to match each requirement to the correct capability:

- "Convert a phone call to text." Speech-to-text.
- "Read a document aloud." Text-to-speech.
- "Translate spoken Spanish into spoken English." Speech translation.

#### Activity 3: Design a Voice Feature

Ask learners to choose one app they use and describe how speech input or output could improve the experience.

### Checks for Understanding

Use these questions during or after instruction:

1. What is AI speech?
2. What is speech recognition?
3. What is speech synthesis?
4. What is the difference between speech-to-text and text-to-speech?
5. Which speech capability is used to transcribe meetings?
6. Which speech capability is used to read an answer aloud?
7. What are examples of common AI speech scenarios?

### Teaching Notes

- Reinforce the input/output distinction: speech recognition is spoken input; speech synthesis is spoken output.
- Use scenario-based examples because AI-901 often asks learners to choose the correct AI capability for a requirement.
- Keep terminology simple: speech-to-text and text-to-speech are the most important patterns for this unit.
- Mention that modern speech systems may need to handle background noise, interruptions, and natural-sounding voices, but do not go deep into implementation.
