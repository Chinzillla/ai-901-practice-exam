# Module 7: Plan and Prepare to Develop AI Solutions on Azure

## Unit 6 Question Bank: Responsible AI

These questions are authored from the unit's Microsoft Learn concepts and rewritten as exam-style practice items. Answer choices are intentionally paraphrased instead of copied from the lesson text.

---

### 1. A team tests whether an AI assistant gives lower-quality answers to users from a particular group. Which principle is involved?

A. responsible AI  
B. transparency  
C. human oversight  
D. Fairness  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** The requirement matches the unit capability named in the correct option.

---

### 2. What is the main purpose of "responsible AI"?

A. making system behavior and limitations understandable to stakeholders  
B. keeping people involved in reviewing and governing AI behavior  
C. building AI systems with fairness, safety, privacy, transparency, inclusiveness, and accountability  
D. checking that an AI system does not unfairly advantage or disadvantage groups  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** "responsible AI" is a core idea in this unit, and the correct option paraphrases its role.

---

### 3. A product page explains that AI answers may be incomplete and should be reviewed. Which principle is supported?

A. Speech synthesis  
B. Transparency capability  
C. Prompt engineering  
D. Object detection  

**Answer:** B  
**Rationale:** The correct option is the unit concept that best fits the stated requirement.

---

### 4. How does responsible AI affect development work?

A. It adds design, testing, monitoring, and governance practices around the AI capability.  
B. It only applies after an app is retired.  
C. It means the model never needs evaluation.  
D. It applies only to image generation.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Responsible AI is part of planning, building, and operating solutions.

---

### 5. What should a learner remember about "fairness"?

A. a configuration task for the interface rather than the AI behavior  
B. a rule-based process that does not use model reasoning  
C. an access-control setting rather than the concept being defined  
D. evaluating whether an AI system does not unfairly advantage or disadvantage groups  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** "fairness" is tested by recognizing its purpose, not by memorizing wording from the page.

---

### 6. Why should responsible AI be considered before deployment?

A. Responsible AI prevents all model use.  
B. It is only a marketing activity.  
C. Risks are easier to reduce when they are identified during design and testing.  
D. Risks cannot be detected until users complain.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** The unit stresses responsible planning, not only reaction after release.

---

### 7. Which two actions support responsible AI development? Choose two.

A. Hide known risks  
B. Ignore user feedback  
C. Remove access controls  
D. Test for harmful behavior  
E. Document model limitations  

**Answer:** D, E  
**Rationale:** Evaluation and transparency reduce risk.

---

### 8. A company assigns owners to monitor AI issues after launch. Which responsible practice is this?

A. Human accountability and oversight  
B. Information extraction  
C. Model evaluation  
D. Choose a model based on popularity instead of task fit.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** The scenario points to the unit capability described by the correct option.

---

### 9. Which three responsible AI ideas are relevant to Azure AI solutions? Choose three.

A. Display resolution  
B. Developer productivity shortcuts  
C. Image file name length  
D. Avoiding unfair treatment across groups  
E. Privacy and security  
F. Accountability  

**Answer:** D, E, F  
**Rationale:** These are responsible AI principles learners should recognize.

### 10. Which description best matches "transparency"?

A. Use a visual analysis capability when the input is only text.  
B. Handle sensitive data without the required access controls.  
C. making limitations and behavior understandable to stakeholders  
D. Rely on one successful trial instead of broader evaluation.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** The correct answer paraphrases how the unit explains "transparency".
