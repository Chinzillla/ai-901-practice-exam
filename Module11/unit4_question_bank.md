### 1. A company needs a model to follow a specialized response style across thousands of similar prompts. Which strategy may help?

A. training examples  
B. consistent behavior  
C. base model

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** The requirement matches the unit capability named in the correct option.

### 2. What is the main purpose of "fine-tuning"?

A. reliable response style or decisions across similar requests  
B. the original model before task-specific adaptation  
C. additional training that adapts a model toward a specific behavior or task  
D. sample inputs and desired outputs used to teach the model a pattern  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** "fine-tuning" is a core idea in this unit, and the correct option paraphrases its role.

A. Speech synthesis  
B. Examples used to adapt behavior

**Rationale:** The correct option is the unit concept that best fits the stated requirement.

### 4. When is fine-tuning a better fit than prompt engineering alone?

A. When many examples are needed to make behavior consistent across repeated task patterns.  
B. When the problem is only missing current facts.  
C. When no training examples are available.  
D. When a simple output-format instruction is enough.  

**Answer:** A  
**Rationale:** Fine-tuning is heavier than prompting and is used for consistent task behavior.

### 5. What should a learner remember about "training examples"?

B. a rule-based process that does not use model reasoning  
C. an access-control setting rather than the concept being defined  
D. training examples used to teach the model a pattern  

**Answer:** D  
**Rationale:** "training examples" is tested by recognizing its purpose, not by memorizing wording from the page.

### 6. Why does fine-tuning require high-quality examples?

A. Low-quality examples always improve output.  
B. Fine-tuning only changes endpoint names.  
C. The model learns from the patterns shown in the training data.  
D. Examples are ignored during fine-tuning.  

**Answer:** C  
**Rationale:** Training data quality directly affects adapted behavior.

### 7. Which two are reasons to consider fine-tuning? Choose two.

**Rationale:** Fine-tuning is for repeated patterns and specialized behavior.

### 9. Which three items matter in a fine-tuning workflow? Choose three.
